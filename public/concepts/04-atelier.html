II

A Note on Method

❖

Most software is bought. The systems described here were built — to the measure of one company at a time, around the way its people actually work. Each one began as a question of operations, not technology: what must this business see, decide, and deliver every day, and what stands in the way.

The practice covers the full span. Requirements are taken first-hand. Platforms are designed, built, and put into service. And they are operated thereafter — watched, maintained, and refined in production, season over season. Nothing is handed off and forgotten.

— A.B.
