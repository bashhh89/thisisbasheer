## Overview

A national sports-technology business was paying for a CRM that didn't fit it. The real work — maintenance logs, design requests, inventory, field walkthroughs — didn't match the contact-deal-task shape, so it lived in notes and side spreadsheets nobody could query.

## The system

A self-hosted CRM platform, extended into the company's operating system:

- **13 first-class objects** modelled on the actual domain — fields, relationships, and views that match how the team thinks
- **Migration with parity** — 30,000+ records moved, then reconciled against the legacy system report by report until leadership trusted the numbers
- **Automation and reporting** — rollups, recurring tasks, scheduled exports, 16 live dashboards
- **AI helpers** — drafting, summarizing, triage, and on-demand reports, scoped to company data and reviewed where it matters

## What changed

The CRM stopped being a place to log things after the fact and became the system the team works in. It syncs with the field-operations platform and receives closed deals from the proposal engine — one record of the business, three systems feeding it.

## Final outcome

Leadership reads live dashboards instead of waiting on exports. New hires ramp in days. The legacy subscription is being retired the right way — beaten on parity first, then switched off.
