## Overview

The team had outgrown the standard CRM playbook. Their work didn't fit the contact-deal-task model — they needed first-class objects for the things they actually managed, with automation that respected how those objects relate.

## The system

The platform is built on an open-source CRM core, extended with:

- Custom objects modelled directly on the business's domain
- Automation rules for the moves the team was making by hand
- Internal tools that surface the right data at the right step
- AI-assisted helpers for drafting, summarizing, and triage

It runs as a single platform with one source of truth.

## Key capabilities

- Domain-specific objects, fields, and relationships
- Workflow automation tied to real-world events
- AI helpers integrated where they save real time
- Permissioned views by role and team
- Clean upgrade path as the upstream CRM evolves

## What changed

The CRM stopped being a place to log things after the fact. It became the system the team works *in*. Onboarding is faster because the system reflects how the work actually happens.

## Final outcome

The business now runs on a CRM that fits it precisely. New requirements are configuration changes, not workarounds — and the AI layer keeps quietly absorbing the work that used to need a senior operator.
