## Overview

A national LED display integrator needed to stop running its sales motion out of spreadsheets. Quoting a stadium project meant days of manual assembly, pricing drifted between reps, and there was no system of record once a proposal was sent.

## The system

Three layers in one Postgres-backed platform:

- **Structured intake** — opportunities arrive as RFPs and legacy spreadsheets; the engine parses them into structured data instead of having them re-typed
- A **versioned catalog** of products, services, and pricing rules — centrally managed, frozen per sent proposal
- A **generator** that assembles client-ready output with consistent language, clean PDFs, and a full audit trail

The engine can reproduce an existing estimate exactly — down to the formatting finance expects — or recalculate from the catalog's rules. Teams migrate at their own pace.

## What changed

The team stopped negotiating with their own spreadsheets. Reps spend their time on the customer instead of the document. Finance trusts the numbers because they come from one place, versioned.

## Final outcome

A proposal that took days now takes under an hour, and every number in it is reproducible. Onboarding a new offering is a configuration change, not a rebuild — and closed deals hand off to the CRM automatically.
